# ANALYSIS GUIDE — the top-down waterfall

This is the spine of the workspace: how the published numbers descend from raw waveforms,
and where the committed evidence for each step lives. If you read one file to decide whether
to trust the result, read this one, then spot-check any row of the provenance table.

## The claim

σ_t = **25.7 ± 0.6 ps** at 150 GeV (DSB1 build, brightest-1000 showers, saturation-recovered
CFD, reference-free (DW−UP)/2); **≈50 ps** (50.5) over the full fiducial sample; fitted floor
**18.8 ± 0.8 ps**, confirming the published 17.5 ps. Cross-build stochastic term
a = 203±6 → 440±18 ps·√GeV (DSB1 → LuAG:Ce); same-shower MIXED width ratio
σ_DSB1/σ_LuAG = **1.04 ± 0.05**; dual-end mean-asymmetry drift **−33.6 ± 2.9 ps per e-fold**.
Wording rules for every public number: `papers/memory_claims_and_forbidden_language.md`.

## The waterfall

```
raw DRS4 waveforms  (data/2023/raw/ subset; full set on Argon/EOS — see README)
   │   reduce/Reducer.C + reduceRun.C   (config-driven; data/2023/configs/<BUILD>.json)
   │   ├─ calibration: reduce/calibHGLG.C → <BUILD>.hglg sidecars (committed)
   │   └─ validation:  reduce/validateReduce.C (bit-identity gate), reduce/verify.C
   ▼
reduced 'rad' ntuples  (data/2023/reduced/<BUILD>/<E>GeV.root — 39-branch schema, lib/io/Schema.h)
   │   selection:  lib/physics/SelectionCuts.h        (every threshold, with WHY comments)
   │   estimators: lib/physics/RadTiming.h            (timingBrightestK + robust tebSigma)
   │               srCFD ≡ the hg_lgcfd branch        (LG-predicted-peak CFD; paper Sec. 3)
   ▼
GATED RESULTS  (papers/scripts/<gate>/ — one dir per published result:
   │            macro + pre-registered AUDIT.md + committed result log)
   ▼
tables  (papers/tables/*.md — generated, single source of truth for the numbers)
   ▼
manuscript  (papers/timing/radical_timing.tex → radical_timing.pdf; figs/ carries the copies)
```

Governance around the waterfall: the **claims law**
(`papers/memory_claims_and_forbidden_language.md`), the **gate history**
(`papers/memory_analysis_gates.md`), and the append-only **campaign snapshot**
(`papers/CAMPAIGN_SNAPSHOT_2026-06-09.md`). Estimator naming bridge: the paper's **srCFD**
is the code's `hg_lgcfd` source (`RadView::kLGCFD`); `cfd05` and `LED` are the diagnostic
sources.

## Exploratory vs load-bearing

`analyze/studies/` (~118 macros) is the preserved exploration record — hypotheses, dead ends,
and the studies that *became* gates. Load-bearing for the paper are only: the generators in
`papers/scripts/*/`, plus `narrativeFigs.C` + `hgLgPlot.C` (Figs. 2–4) from studies.
Deprecated studies carry in-file banners (e.g. `mixedHeadToHead.C`, `paperSystematics.C`);
if a study contradicts a gated product, the gate wins — see
`papers/memory_analysis_gates.md` for what was retired and why (including the honest
retirement of the 0.99 MIXED ratio).

## History and integrity

The campaign is append-only: `papers/CAMPAIGN_SNAPSHOT_2026-06-09.md` records each pass
(estimator-bug fix, gate closures, prior-record audit, formatting passes) with commands and
commit hashes. Nothing in the published chain was backdated; the one post-hoc audit
(`full_fiducial_check/AUDIT.md`) says so on its face.
