The challenge

Why timing, and why now

  • HL-LHC & future colliders (FCC-ee/hh, fixed-target, forward physics): up to ~200 overlapping interactions per crossing, and intense radiation.
  • Space alone no longer separates them — reconstruction needs time, stamping each shower to a few tens of ps.
  • A calorimeter that measures energy + time + position together — "4D" — is a central instrumentation goal.
  • The materials must be radiation hard and fast, and we must learn which ones work.

The concept

Sample the light where the shower is brightest

  • An EM shower peaks at shower maximum — a small, dense, prompt burst of energy.
  • RADiCAL reads the scintillation light right there, through wavelength-shifting (WLS) filaments inside quartz capillaries that pipe it to SiPMs.
  • A compact, localized, very bright optical pulse → an excellent timing signal, and a handle on position and energy.
  • Index-finger-sized module; 25 radiation lengths deep.
Construction

How it is built

  • Shashlik stack: 28 tungsten (W) absorber plates interleaved with 29 LYSO:Ce scintillator plates — 25 X₀, 14 × 14 × 135 mm³.
  • Quartz capillaries penetrate the full length at the corners; each carries a WLS filament and acts as a waveguide.
  • SiPMs at both ends (upstream + downstream), each in dual gain: high gain → timing, low gain → energy.
  • Digitized at 5 GS/s (CAEN DT5742 / DRS4); an MCP gives a precision time reference.
The capillaries

Two jobs, two capillary types

T-type Timing. WLS filament only at shower max → a sharp, bright, localized pulse. Fast leading edge.
E-type Energy. WLS runs the full length → a uniform longitudinal response. Linear in energy.
  • Read at both ends → the up/down difference cancels the reference and exposes intrinsic timing.
  • Same module, swappable inserts — the basis of the standard-candle method.
Tshower-max WLS → timing
Efull-length WLS → energy
Deploy T-type and E-type together → time, position and energy from one module.
The idea

RADiCAL as a standard candle

  • Fix a standard reference configuration (DSB1 T-type capillaries) and measure its baseline: timing, energy, position.
  • Swap one element at a time — a capillary material, an electronics card, a scintillator plate — and measure the delta vs the standard.
  • The module, beam, geometry and analysis are held fixed, so the change isolates the effect of that material.
  • A reproducible, head-to-head test bed for the whole community's optical materials.

How we measure

An MCP-free corner estimator

  • (DW − UP)/2: the half-difference of downstream and upstream capillary times.
  • Cancels the timing reference, the inter-group sampler jitter, and the DRS4 cell-width error — exposes the intrinsic module resolution.
  • srCFD time-stamping — a constant fraction of the low-gain-predicted true peak, so clipped pulses are timed on their steep edge (LED for the dim builds).
  • Deterministic brightest-1000 selection — fixed before any fit, so no optimization bias; quoted beside the full-fiducial ≈50 ps.
Results · timing

The standard: 25.7 ps at 150 GeV

25.7 ps150 GeV · DSB1 · brightest-1000
  • ≈50 ps across the full fiducial sample — the baseline every material is compared against.
  • MCP-free; deterministic selection, no optimization bias.
  • Fitted floor 18.8 ± 0.8 ps confirms the published 17.5 ps; its origin is shower-depth fluctuation (−33.6 ps/e-fold), not the DAQ.

Results · energy

Shower-max energy, build-independent

  • Energy from the low-gain (unsaturated) chain at shower max.
  • ~14% — a localized shower-max measurement (≈3 LYSO tiles), not full containment.
  • Full containment → a 3 × 3 array drives the stochastic term toward 10%/√E.

Results · the E-type capillary

A dedicated, linear energy element

  • Full-length WLS → uniform longitudinal response (the energy element).
  • Linear in beam energy; single-channel timing comparable to T-type.
  • Deploy E-type + T-type together → the full 4D module.

The road to 10 ps

More light, sharper edges

  • Timing precision ≈ noise / slew rate — and what sharpens the edge is more detected photoelectrons.
  • Levers: brighter (and rad-hard) WLS & scintillators · thicker LYSO at shower max · better photodetectors · lower-noise, faster electronics.
  • We have shown the WLS species is not the limit — light yield is the lever.
  • Bridging today's 25.7 ps toward the 10 ps goal is a light-yield-and-electronics program, well-matched to this test bed.
Plans

What's next

  • LuO:Yb ceramic plates — radiation-hard scintillator replacing LYSO:Ce.
  • LuAG:Ce & LuO:Yb capillaries — push the light yield of rad-hard shifters.
  • Enhanced modular design — T-type + E-type together; updated front-end & DAQ.
  • 3 × 3 array — full transverse containment → energy resolution toward 10%/√E.
  • Drive the timing toward the 10 ps goal — more light and faster, lower-noise electronics.

Summary

RADiCAL — a standard candle for fast-timing calorimetry

  • An index-finger-sized W/LYSO:Ce module reads shower-max light → time, energy, position.
  • A fixed standard + one-knob-at-a-time swaps = a controlled materials test bed.
  • To date: 25.7 ps timing (brightest-1000; ≈50 ps full fiducial), ~14% shower-max energy, ~1.5 mm position, and a 4D demonstration.
  • Timing is set by light yield, not WLS species → radiation-hard ceramics are on the path.
  • Goal: 10 ps — and we want collaborators to get there.
